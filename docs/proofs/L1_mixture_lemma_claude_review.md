## 1. Fatal Gaps

### F1: Step 3 Does Not Follow from A-001 — Hidden Exclusion Restriction

**Confidence: HIGH**

#### The Problem

A-001 (as registered) states: "observed choices `Y_it` are generated by a latent decision parameter `theta_i` and observed trial features `X_it` through `p(Y_it | X_it, theta_i; eta)`." This is a structural statement about the choice mechanism.

Lemma 1.1's Step 3 claims: "By A-001, once `X` and `Θ` are given, the probability of the choice is represented by the model kernel: `P(Y = y | X = x, Z = z, Θ = θ) = p(y | x,θ; η*)`."

The passage from the former to the latter requires:
```
Y ⊥⊥ (Z, C, U) | (X, Θ),
```

That is: conditional on `(X, Θ)`, the outcome `Y` is independent of all omitted context `Z`, `C`, and latent heterogeneity `U`. Equivalently, `Z`, `C`, and `U` affect `Y` only through their effect on `Θ` (given `X`), and the choice shock is fresh noise independent of everything else.

#### Why This Matters

The derivation's claim "By A-001…" is a **non sequitur**. If, say, `U` leaked directly into the choice rule—
```
Y | X,Θ,U ~ Bern(logit⁻¹(Θ + U))
```
while the registered kernel is `logit⁻¹(θ)`—then A-001's registered text might be satisfied under a loose reading, but the hypothesis would fail:
```
P(Y=1|X,Z,Θ=θ) = E[logit⁻¹(θ+U)] ≠ logit⁻¹(θ).
```

The mixture identity would be false for the registered kernel. This proves the exclusion restriction is genuinely restrictive, not merely notational.

#### Formal Restatement

The lemma itself (as written in Lemma 1.1) is internally valid: it states the hypothesis directly and derives the consequence. The **derivation** in Step 3 is what fails. The draft claims to derive the hypothesis from A-001, but the inference is incomplete.

#### Governance Implication

Sharpening A-001 (or adding the restriction as a new assumption A-001′) plausibly triggers the "adding new theorem assumptions" human-approval gate in the repository's procedure-compliance rules. This cannot be silently patched by the autonomous loop; it requires PI decision.

### F2: Multi-Trial Application Requires Serial Noise Independence (Not Stated)

**Confidence: HIGH that it is needed; MEDIUM that it will be classified as fatal vs. scope-limiting**

#### The Problem

The lemma is stated as a single-observation identity:
```
P(Y = y | X = x, Z = z) = ∫ p(y | x,θ; η*) dF_{Θ | X,Z}(θ | x,z).
```

But §1 and §6 immediately deploy it as the target of model comparison on `(i,t)`-indexed data where `Θ = θ_i` is shared across trials `t`. Two consequences follow:

**Consequence A: Within-participant choices require serial independence.**  
For the per-trial marginal law `P(Y_it | X_it, Z_i) = ∫ p(y_it | x_it, θ_i; η*) dF(θ_i | ...)` to hold even under adaptive trial designs, the choice shock at trial `t` must be independent of the design history and previous choices, given `θ_i`. The draft does not state this assumption.

**Consequence B: The joint within-participant law does NOT factorize.**  
In general:
```
∫ ∏_t p(y_t|x_t,θ) dF(θ)  ≠  ∏_t ∫ p(y_t|x_t,θ) dF(θ).
```

That is, the participant-level joint mixture does not factor into a product of trial-level marginal mixtures. Therefore:

- Any downstream result **evaluated at the trial level** with a **product criterion** (e.g., sum of trial log-likelihoods, where expected log score is linear in trials) may target `p_0` directly by linearity.
- Any downstream result **evaluated at the participant level** (e.g., participant-level LOO, participant-level Bayes factors) must be derived from the **joint mixture** `∫ ∏_t p(y_t|x_t,θ) dF(θ)`, not from products of marginals.

#### Why This Matters

The draft's §1 claims: "The later Gaussian heterogeneity, KL dominance, and finite-sample selection results all build on this point: model comparison is performed against the context-omitted marginal response law, not against the conditional law that would be visible if the omitted context were modeled."

This is true **only for trial-wise product criteria**. For participant-level predictive targets (like the hierarchical LOO described in Results 4–5 of the current project context), the statement is **false as written**. The lemma does not justify comparing models at the participant level without deriving the joint mixture from it first.

#### Current Project Context

The repository's `docs/current_project_context.md` mentions Result 4 (LOOIC) and does not explicitly specify whether `G` (the held-out unit) is a trial or participant. If participants are the held-out unit, the target is the joint mixture, not the trial marginals. If trials are the held-out unit, the lemma's marginals are sufficient. This ambiguity must be resolved before the lemma's scope is finalized.

## 2. Nonfatal Gaps

### N1: Kernel Measurability Missing from Assumption 4

**Severity: Low; Constraint requirement:**  "Identify all regularity assumptions needed for conditional distributions"

Existence of `F_{Θ|X,Z}` (stated in Assumption 4) is necessary for the disintegration. The derivation's Step 2 additionally requires that the kernel be **jointly measurable in its arguments**:

For each `y ∈ Y_space`, the map `(x,θ) ↦ p(y|x,θ;η*)` must be jointly Borel measurable.

**Why stated:** Integrability is free (bounded in [0,1]), and measurability holds for every kernel the project will actually use (logit, softmax, etc.). But the project constraints explicitly ask to "identify all regularity assumptions needed," and this is one.

**Fix (E3):** Extend Assumption 4 with a clause specifying joint measurability of the kernel.

---

### N2: Assumption 3 Is a Theorem, Not an Assumption

**Severity: Low; Category error**

Listing the "conditional law of total probability" as an assumption is incorrect. This is a consequence of the tower property and disintegration theorem—facts of measure theory, not modeling choices.

**What should happen instead:** 
- Remove Assumption 3 from §3.
- In Step 2 of the derivation, cite the tower property (conditional expectation) and the disintegration theorem (Kallenberg, Theorem 6.3; Durrett, §4.1.3—verify the exact reference against the edition before SI insertion).

This also makes the assumption list cleaner: all remaining items are modeling choices, not probability facts.

---

### N3: Version and Quantifier Hygiene

**Severity: Very low; Exposition**

Three minor points:

1. `P(Y=y|X=x,Z=z,Θ=θ)` is defined only up to null sets of its conditioning event. The hypothesis should read: "for some (equivalently, every) version of the conditional probability, a.s."

2. The `(X,Z)`-a.e. qualifier is correctly present, but the statement quantifies "for any outcome `y`" without noting that the null set can be chosen uniformly over `y` because `Y_space` is finite. This is exactly why the finite-outcome restriction in §7 is load-bearing.

3. The phrase "a.s." appears but should be explicit about with respect to which measure (the product of the marginal of `(X,Z)` and the regular conditional of `Θ` given `(X,Z)`).

**Fix (E5):** Restate the hypothesis and add a remark about finite-outcome hygiene.

---

### N4: "The Probability the Analyst Can Estimate" Overreaches

**Severity: Low; Scope creep**

Step 1 phrasing: "This is the chance of observing choice `y` among observations with trial features `x` and observed covariates `z`. It does not condition on `C`, because `C` is omitted. It also does not condition on the realized value of `Theta`, because `Theta` is latent."

The phrasing "probability the analyst **can estimate**" smuggles in identification assumptions (positivity, sampling design) outside the lemma's scope. The lemma is a **population identity**; consistent estimation requires additional sampling and design assumptions.

**Fix (E6):** Rephrase as "the population conditional law identified by the observed-data distribution; consistent estimation additionally requires sampling and positivity conditions outside this lemma."

---

### N5: Corollary 1.2's Fixed Design Is Defined Circularly

**Severity: Low; Exposition; affects interpretation**

The parenthetical remark in Corollary 1.2 defines "fixed design" as: "`X` is treated as a fixed design" or "carries no information about `Θ` beyond `Z`." But this latter clause is the **conclusion** of the conditional-independence argument, not an independent definition.

More importantly, the structural definition is loose. A fixed-design rule can in principle condition on unrecorded baseline variables that correlate with `U` or `C`. The definition should be:

"Fixed or exogenous trial design: `X` is a measurable function of `(Z, V)` where the randomization device `V` satisfies `V ⊥⊥ (C,U) | Z`."

This makes clear what is and is not allowed.

**Fix (E7):** Provide the structural definition.

---

### N6: A-002 Is Interpretive, Not Load-Bearing

**Severity: Very low; Register cleanliness**

The assumption list in §3 includes A-002 ("omitted context shifts latent parameter: `θ_i = h(Z_i, C_i, U_i)`") and marks it "used." In fact, the identity in Lemma 1.1 holds **whether or not** `C` affects `Θ`. A-002 enters only in Step 4, where the meaning of the mixture is explained: "if `C` has an effect, then part of the spread of `F_{Θ|X,Z}` is context-induced."

**Fix (E8):** Annotate A-002 as "interpretive only; not load-bearing for the identity in Lemma 1.1" and mirror this in the assumption register's dependency column.

## 3. Hidden Assumptions (Load-Bearing Order)

1. **Exclusion restriction** (F1): `Y ⊥⊥ (Z,C,U) | (X,Θ)` — required for Step 3, not stated in current A-001.

2. **Serial choice-noise independence** (F2): Given `Θ` and design history, choice shocks are independent across trials — required for multi-trial lemma use, not stated.

3. **Kernel joint measurability** (N1): The map `(x,θ) ↦ p(y|x,θ;η*)` is Borel measurable for each `y` — required for the integral in Step 2, not stated in Assumption 4.

4. **Fixed parameters:** `η*` is constant across participants and trials and equal to the true parameter — implicit in "fixed parameters," worth one clarifying sentence.

5. **Measurement assumptions:** `Z` measured without error; `C` fully omitted rather than partially proxied by a proxy variable — not needed for the identity itself, but §6's interpretation silently assumes them.

6. **Exogeneity for Corollary 1.2's fixed-design branch:** The assignment rule `X` uses no latent-correlated baseline information beyond `Z` — stated but needs structural definition (N5).

## 4. Counterexample Attempts

### CE-1: Direct Leakage of Latent Heterogeneity into Choice Shock (Attacks Step 3; Succeeds)

**Status:** Demonstrates that the exclusion restriction is genuine, not notation.

**Setup:**
- Let `Θ = C` (context is the latent parameter).
- Registered kernel: `p(1|x,θ) = logit⁻¹(θ)`.
- True choice rule: `Y | X,Θ,U ~ Bern(logit⁻¹(Θ + U))` with `U ~ N(0,1)` independent of `(X,Z,Θ)`.

**Analysis:** A-001's registered text ("choices generated by `theta` through `p(·|·,theta)`") is arguably satisfied under a loose reading. However:
```
P(Y=1|X,Z,Θ=θ) = E[logit⁻¹(θ+U)] = E[1/(1 + exp(-(θ+U)))] ≠ logit⁻¹(θ),
```

The mixture identity with the registered kernel is false. The exclusion restriction is violated: `U` leaks directly into the outcome given `Θ`.

**Conclusion:** This counterexample proves that the exclusion restriction is genuinely restrictive. The derivation cannot proceed from A-001's current phrasing without it.

---

### CE-2: Kernel Redefinition on a Null Set (Attacks N3; Fails)

**Status:** Lemma is robust.

**Setup:** Redefine the kernel `p(y|x,θ;η*)` on a `Θ`-null set that has positive measure under `F_{Θ|X,Z}` for measure-zero `(x,z)`.

**Analysis:** Pointwise equality breaks down at those `(x,z)` values. However, Lemma 1.1's conclusion is stated "for `(X,Z)`-a.e. `(x,z)`," which discards precisely such measure-zero sets.

**Conclusion:** The a.e. qualifier defuses this attack. The lemma is robust to null-set pathology.

---

### CE-3: Adaptive Trial Design (Attacks Corollary 1.2; Kills Corollary, Not Lemma)

**Status:** Already anticipated in §8; no new gap found.

**Setup:** `X_t` is assigned from past choices, making `X_t` a descendant of `Θ`.

**Analysis:** Then `Θ` and `X` are not independent given `Z`, so `F_{Θ|X,Z} ≠ F_{Θ|Z}` and Corollary 1.2's simplified form fails.

**However:** Lemma 1.1 itself survives provided trial-`t` choice noise is fresh given `Θ` and the past (this is where F2's serial-independence assumption is exposed: without it, even the primary statement fails under adaptivity).

**Conclusion:** Corollary 1.2 correctly scopes to fixed/exogenous designs. Lemma 1.1's primary statement is more robust than the corollary. The draft anticipates this correctly in §8 ("adaptive trial designs").

---

### CE-4: Degenerate Mixture (Attacks Lemma generality; Fails)

**Status:** Boundary case correctly handled.

**Setup:** `C` has no effect on `Θ`, and `U` is degenerate (no idiosyncratic heterogeneity).

**Analysis:** Then `F_{Θ|X,Z}` is a point mass at `θ(z)`, and the identity holds trivially.

**Conclusion:** §6.4 correctly states this boundary case. The lemma makes no overreach claim that the mixture is always non-degenerate.

---

### CE-5: Non-Borel Θ-Space (Attacks Assumption 4; Fails)

**Status:** Assumption 4 excludes this case.

**Setup:** `Θ` is not Borel measurable (pathological).

**Analysis:** Then regular conditional distributions may not exist. Assumption 4 requires `Θ` to be Borel/Polish.

**Conclusion:** The project's finite-dimensional real-valued `Θ` automatically satisfies this. No new gap.

## 5. Verdict: REVISE

### Summary of Decision

The mathematical core is sound:
- The tower-property and disintegration argument is correct.
- The a.e. statement is properly hedged.
- The corollary demotion to fixed/exogenous designs respects the A-006 rejection.

**However, three issues block acceptance:**

1. **F1 (fatal):** Step 3 claims an inference from A-001 that is logically invalid without an exclusion restriction. This is repairable by sharpening A-001 to A-001′.

2. **F2 (fatal):** The multi-trial application claimed in §1/§6 overreaches without stating serial-noise independence and without deriving the joint mixture for participant-level predictive targets. This is repairable by adding an assumption and scoping statements.

3. **N1 (nonfatal):** Kernel measurability is not stated in Assumption 4, violating the project constraint to identify all regularity assumptions. This is repairable by one clause.

None of these require new mathematics, only clarification of existing assumptions and scope statements.

## Appendix: Formal Skeleton of the Corrected Argument

For reference, here is the corrected proof in concise form:

**Lemma (Corrected):** Let Y, X, Z, Θ be as defined. Assume:
- (A-001′) Y ⊥⊥ (Z,C,U) | (X,Θ); equivalently, E[1{Y=y} | X,Z,Θ] = p(y|X,Θ;η*) a.s.
- (A-4) Regular conditional F_{Θ|X,Z} exists; kernel (x,θ) ↦ p(y|x,θ;η*) is jointly measurable.

Then for (X,Z)-a.e. (x,z):
```
P(Y=y | X=x, Z=z) 
  = E[1{Y=y} | X=x, Z=z]                          (definition)
  = E[E[1{Y=y} | X=x, Z=z, Θ] | X=x, Z=z]        (tower property)
  = E[p(y | X=x, Θ; η*) | X=x, Z=z]              (by A-001′)
  = ∫ p(y | x, θ; η*) dF_{Θ|X,Z}(θ | x,z)       (disintegration; A-4 measurability)
  =: p_0(y | x, z).                               (definition of p_0)
```

For multi-trial use (participant i, trials t):
```
P(Y_i = y_i | X_i = x_i, Z_i = z_i)
  = ∫ ∏_t P(Y_{it} = y_{it} | X_{it} = x_{it}, Z_i = z_i, Θ_i = θ)  dF_{Θ_i|X_i,Z_i}(θ|x_i,z_i)
  = ∫ ∏_t p(y_{it} | x_{it}, θ; η*) dF_{Θ_i|X_i,Z_i}(θ|x_i,z_i).
```

This is the participant-level joint mixture; it does **not** factorize into products of trial-level marginals.

**End of Formal Skeleton**
